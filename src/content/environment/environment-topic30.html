Conceptual Clarity — Why this Topic Matters

Environmental schemes are among the most frequently and directly tested items in the Environment paper — almost every year has a scheme-matching question. Three patterns recur:

  • Definitional / static: "which Mission is under NAPCC?", nodal ministry of a scheme, launch year, target (e.g. NCAP’s PM reduction target). Pure recall — a scheme↔ministry↔objective table settles it.
  • Statement-elimination (2–4 statements): matching scheme↔feature↔ministry — e.g. Namami Gange is under Jal Shakti, Green India Mission is one of the 8 NAPCC missions, CAMPA funds compensatory afforestation. One wrong pairing sinks the option.
  • Applied / current: newly launched or rebranded schemes (Mission LiFE, Amrit Dharohar, MISHTI, revised FAME/PM E-DRIVE), budget outlays, targets missed/met. Mains GS-III links schemes to climate commitments (Panchamrit/net-zero 2070) & governance.

Highest-frequency themes: NAPCC 8 missions · Namami Gange · NCAP target · Green India Mission & CAMPA · Project Tiger/Elephant · Swachh Bharat & GOBARdhan · Mission LiFE.

1.

Classification of Schemes & the Ministries Behind Them

Before the individual schemes, fix the vocabulary and the ownership map — UPSC loves testing which ministry runs which programme, and whether something is a “Mission,” a “Scheme” or a fund.

1.1 Mission vs Scheme vs Programme vs Fund

TypeMeaningExample
MissionTime-bound, target-driven, usually under a national plan with dedicated structure.National Solar Mission, Green India Mission.
SchemeA funded intervention (CSS = Centrally Sponsored, or CS = Central Sector).Namami Gange, FAME.
ProgrammeBroad umbrella of activities, often non-time-bound.National Clean Air Programme (NCAP).
FundFinancing pool, not a scheme itself.CAMPA, National Clean Energy & Environment Fund.

Green India Mission, CAMPAMoEFCCForest & tree cover.
Project Tiger / ElephantMoEFCC — NTCASpecies & habitat.

2.

NAPCC & its 8 National Missions

The National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC), 2008 is India’s core climate framework, run through 8 National Missions. States prepare complementary State Action Plans on Climate Change (SAPCC).

MissionLead MinistryKey thrust / example
National Solar Mission (JNNSM)MNREGrid + rooftop solar; feeds the 500 GW-by-2030 RE target.
Enhanced Energy EfficiencyPower (BEE)PAT scheme, energy-saving certificates, star labelling.
Sustainable HabitatMoHUAEnergy-efficient buildings (ECBC), urban transport, waste.
Water MissionJal Shakti20% water-use efficiency; links to Jal Jeevan/Atal Bhujal.
Sustaining Himalayan EcosystemScience & TechnologyGlacier monitoring, mountain ecology.
Green India Mission (GIM)MoEFCCIncrease forest/tree cover on 5 m ha + improve quality on another 5 m ha.
Sustainable AgricultureAgricultureClimate-resilient crops, soil health, water efficiency.
Strategic Knowledge for Climate ChangeScience & TechnologyClimate research, modelling, data.
Memory hook: “SE-HW-GAS” — Solar, Energy-efficiency, Habitat, Water, Himalayan, Green India, Agriculture, Strategic-knowledge. Only 8 — a “Health Mission” or “Wind Mission” in an option is the trap.
3.

Namami Gange & River-Cleaning Programmes

Launched 2014 as an Integrated Ganga Conservation Mission, implemented by the National Mission for Clean Ganga (NMCG) under the Ministry of Jal Shakti, with a five-tier structure down to District Ganga Committees.

3.1 Pillars of Namami Gange

PillarWhat it doesExample
Sewerage treatmentBuild/upgrade STPs on a Hybrid Annuity (PPP) model.STPs at Varanasi, Kanpur.
River-front & ghat developmentClean, redevelop ghats & crematoria.Ghats in Haridwar.
River-surface cleaningSkimmers remove floating solid waste.Trash skimmers.
Afforestation & biodiversityRiverbank plantation; protect Gangetic dolphin, gharial, turtles.Ganga Prahari volunteers.
Public participation & industrial monitoringGRBMP, online effluent monitoring of GPIs (grossly polluting industries).Real-time CETP data.
  • Structure: National Ganga Council (chaired by PM) → Empowered Task Force → NMCG → State Programme Management Groups → District Ganga Committees.
  • Legal backing: National Ganga Council notified under the EPA 1986 (2016).
  • Recognition: named among the UN’s Top 10 World Restoration Flagships (2022).
Related: the National River Conservation Plan (NRCP) covers other rivers; the Gangetic river dolphin is the National Aquatic Animal and Project Dolphin (2020) protects it.
4.

Green India Mission & Afforestation Schemes

A cluster of programmes to expand forest & tree cover, feed India’s NDC carbon-sink target (an additional 2.5–3 billion tonnes CO₂e sink by 2030) and green cities.

SchemeMinistry / BodyPurpose
Green India Mission (GIM)MoEFCC (NAPCC Mission 6)Increase forest/tree cover on 5 m ha & improve quality on 5 m ha; enhance ecosystem services.
CAMPAMoEFCC (2016 Act)Compensatory Afforestation Fund — collects afforestation + NPV money from diverted forest land.
Nagar Van YojanaMoEFCC (2020)Create urban forests/parks on city land (target 1000 Nagar Vans).
National Afforestation ProgrammeMoEFCCAfforestation of degraded forests via JFMCs.
MISHTIMoEFCC (2023)Mangrove Initiative for Shoreline Habitats & Tangible Incomes — mangrove restoration.
Data hook: India’s forest & tree cover trends are reported in the India State of Forest Report (ISFR) by FSI, released biennially check latest. GIM feeds the NDC forestry sink goal.
Fig 30.2 — The five operational pillars of Namami Gange under NMCG (Jal Shakti): sewage treatment, ghats, surface cleaning, biodiversity and industry/public participation.
5.

National Clean Air Programme (NCAP)

Launched 2019 by MoEFCC as India’s first national, time-bound strategy to reduce particulate pollution across non-attainment cities.

FeatureDetail
TargetOriginally 20–30% cut in PM2.5/PM10 by 2024 (base 2017); revised to 40% reduction (or National Standards) by 2025-26.
Coverage131 non-attainment cities (exceeding national ambient air-quality standards) + Million-Plus cities.
FundingCity action plans; large cities funded via the 15th Finance Commission air-quality grants.
TrackingPRANA portal monitors implementation.
Linked schemesCommission for Air Quality Management (CAQM) for NCR; SAMEER app; GRAP (Graded Response Action Plan).
Prelims trap: NCAP is a programme, not a legally-mandated Act; “non-attainment cities” are those that failed to meet ambient standards over 5 years. NCAP is under MoEFCC/CPCB, distinct from the NCR-specific CAQM (a statutory body under its own 2021 Act).

Wildlife & Species Conservation Programmes

Flagship species programmes anchor India’s conservation success stories — high-frequency in Prelims for their year, nodal body and target species.

ProgrammeYear / BodyFocus
Project Tiger1973; NTCATiger reserves; core-buffer model. India holds ~75% of wild tigers.
Project Elephant1992; MoEFCCElephant reserves, corridors, MIKE; “Gaj Yatra.”
Project Dolphin2020; MoEFCCGangetic (river) & marine dolphins.
Project Lion2020; MoEFCCAsiatic lion (Gir); “Lion@2047.”
Project Cheetah2022; NTCAReintroduction from Namibia/South Africa (Kuno NP).
IDWHMoEFCCIntegrated Development of Wildlife Habitats — umbrella funding scheme (includes recovery of 22+ critically endangered species).
CAMPA-funded species work, Sea Turtle, Vulture, Snow Leopard (Project) & Great Indian BustardMoEFCCRecovery programmes for threatened species.
Note: Project Tiger & Project Cheetah are administered through the NTCA; the NTCA is a statutory body under the Wildlife (Protection) Act (2006 amendment). Tiger Census is released every 4 years check latest.

Swachh Bharat, Waste & Bioenergy Schemes

Sanitation and waste-to-wealth programmes with a direct environmental payoff — open-defecation-free status, waste segregation, and turning organic/agri waste into energy.

SchemeMinistryObjective / example
Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin)Jal ShaktiODF & ODF-Plus villages; solid/liquid waste management. Launched 2014.
Swachh Bharat Mission (Urban 2.0)MoHUAGarbage-free cities, source segregation, legacy-waste remediation.
GOBARdhanJal ShaktiGalvanizing Organic Bio-Agro Resources — convert cattle dung/organic waste to biogas/CBG.
SATATPetroleum & Natural GasSustainable Alternative Towards Affordable Transportation — Compressed Bio-Gas (CBG) plants.
Waste to EnergyMNREPower/biogas from urban, industrial & agri waste.
Plastic / EPR portalsMoEFCC/CPCBSUP ban enforcement; EPR registration for producers.
Link: GOBARdhan & SATAT feed the ethanol/CBG blending push and the circular-economy agenda; the biogas/CBG produced counts toward India’s bioenergy targets (see Topic 26).
8.

Energy, Solar & E-Mobility Schemes

Scheme-focused view of the clean-energy transition — the launch year, ministry and target of each is prime Prelims material.

SchemeMinistry / YearObjective
National Solar Mission (JNNSM)MNRE, 2010Grid + rooftop + off-grid solar; feeds 500 GW RE by 2030.
PM-KUSUMMNRE, 2019Solar pumps & grid-connected solar for farmers (Components A/B/C).
PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli YojanaMNRE, 2024Rooftop solar for 1 crore households, free units.
National Green Hydrogen MissionMNRE, 20235 MMT green hydrogen by 2030.
FAME I & II / PM E-DRIVEHeavy IndustriesFaster Adoption & Manufacturing of EVs; demand incentives, charging infra. FAME II (2019); PM E-DRIVE (2024) succeeds it.
PLI (Solar PV, ACC batteries)MNRE / Heavy IndustriesProduction-Linked Incentives for domestic manufacturing.
Prelims trap: FAME is under the Ministry of Heavy Industries, NOT MoRTH or MNRE. PM-KUSUM has three components: A (grid solar on barren land), B (standalone solar pumps), C (solarising grid-connected pumps).

Wetlands, Coasts & Mission LiFE

The newest cluster — wetland & coastal restoration plus the behavioural-change flagship India projected globally at COP.

SchemeYear / BodyFocus
Amrit Dharohar2023; MoEFCCPromote optimal use of wetlands, enhance biodiversity & eco-tourism at Ramsar sites.
MISHTI2023; MoEFCCMangrove restoration along the coastline & salt-marsh lands.
National Plan for Conservation of Aquatic Ecosystems (NPCA)MoEFCCMerged wetlands + lakes conservation schemes.
Wetlands (Amrit Dharohar) / Ramsar driveMoEFCCIndia crossed 80+ Ramsar sites — most in Asia check latest.
Mission LiFE2022 (COP27); MoEFCC“Lifestyle for Environment” — nudge individual pro-planet behaviour; “LiFE” added to Panchamrit narrative.
Mains hook: Mission LiFE reframes climate action from top-down targets to mindful consumption — India pushed the “pro-planet people (P3)” idea at COP26/27 and into the G20 (2023) outcomes. Use it to argue demand-side climate mitigation.
10.

Current Affairs & Recent Developments

Schemes are re-branded, extended or re-funded almost every Budget — these dated hooks are the highest-yield “applied” material.

  • 2024 PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana launched — rooftop solar for 1 crore households (MNRE).
  • 2024 PM E-DRIVE notified, succeeding FAME-II for EV demand incentives & charging infrastructure (Heavy Industries).
  • 2023 Amrit Dharohar & MISHTI announced in the Union Budget — wetland & mangrove restoration (MoEFCC).
  • 2023 National Green Hydrogen Mission approved (5 MMT by 2030).
  • 2023 GOBARdhan revamped with a unified registration portal for CBG/biogas plants.
  • 2022 Mission LiFE launched at COP27; carried into the G20 New Delhi Declaration (2023).
  • 2022 Project Cheetah — first cheetahs reintroduced at Kuno National Park.
  • Ongoing NCAP target revised to 40% PM reduction by 2025-26; tracked on the PRANA portal check latest.
Exam angle: expect a statement-matching Prelims Q on scheme↔year↔ministry (especially the 2023–24 launches) and a Mains hook linking Mission LiFE / green-hydrogen to India’s net-zero-2070 & Panchamrit commitments.

11. Prelims PYQs

UPSC Prelims 2015

Q: Which of the following is NOT one of the eight National Missions under the National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC)?

  • (a) National Water Mission
  • (b) National Mission for a Green India
  • (c) National Wind Energy Mission
  • (d) National Solar Mission

Ans: (c) — The 8 NAPCC missions are Solar, Enhanced Energy Efficiency, Sustainable Habitat, Water, Sustaining Himalayan Ecosystem, Green India, Sustainable Agriculture and Strategic Knowledge. There is no separate “Wind Energy Mission.”

UPSC Prelims 2018

Q: The Namami Gange programme is implemented by which body under which ministry?

  • (a) CPCB under MoEFCC
  • (b) NMCG under the Ministry of Jal Shakti
  • (c) NTCA under MoEFCC
  • (d) NITI Aayog

Ans: (b) — The National Mission for Clean Ganga (NMCG) implements Namami Gange under the Ministry of Jal Shakti; the National Ganga Council is chaired by the Prime Minister.

UPSC Prelims 2021

Q: Consider the following about the National Clean Air Programme (NCAP):  1. It is a statutory programme mandated by an Act of Parliament.  2. It targets non-attainment cities.  3. Implementation is tracked via the PRANA portal.  Which are correct?

  • (a) 1 and 2 only
  • (b) 2 and 3 only
  • (c) 1 and 3 only
  • (d) 1, 2 and 3

Ans: (b) — NCAP is a policy programme (not a statutory Act); it targets non-attainment cities and is tracked on PRANA. Statement 1 is wrong.

UPSC Prelims 2024

Q: The scheme “MISHTI” launched in 2023 is associated with:

  • (a) Millet promotion
  • (b) Mangrove restoration
  • (c) Micro-irrigation
  • (d) Solar rooftops

Ans: (b) — MISHTI = Mangrove Initiative for Shoreline Habitats & Tangible Incomes; Amrit Dharohar (announced alongside) targets wetlands.

UPSC Prelims 2020

Q: The FAME scheme for electric vehicles is administered by which ministry?

  • (a) Ministry of Road Transport & Highways
  • (b) Ministry of New & Renewable Energy
  • (c) Ministry of Heavy Industries
  • (d) Ministry of Power

Ans: (c) — FAME (and its successor PM E-DRIVE) is run by the Ministry of Heavy Industries — a frequent trap since students assume MoRTH or MNRE.

UPSC Prelims 2017

Q: Match the scheme with its nodal ministry:  1. GOBARdhan — Jal Shakti  2. PM-KUSUM — MNRE  3. Green India Mission — MoEFCC.  Which pairs are correctly matched?

  • (a) 1 and 2 only
  • (b) 2 and 3 only
  • (c) 1 and 3 only
  • (d) 1, 2 and 3

Ans: (d) — All three are correct: GOBARdhan (Jal Shakti), PM-KUSUM (MNRE), Green India Mission (MoEFCC, a NAPCC mission).

UPSC Prelims 2023

Q: Mission LiFE, launched by India, is best described as:

  • (a) A river-interlinking programme
  • (b) A global initiative promoting sustainable, pro-planet lifestyles
  • (c) A wildlife-corridor scheme
  • (d) A carbon-market mechanism

Ans: (b) — Mission LiFE (Lifestyle for Environment), launched at COP27 (2022), promotes mindful, demand-side climate action — carried into the G20 New Delhi Declaration.

UPSC Prelims 2022

Q: Project Cheetah, involving reintroduction of cheetahs to India, is implemented at:

  • (a) Gir National Park
  • (b) Bandhavgarh National Park
  • (c) Kuno National Park
  • (d) Ranthambore National Park

Ans: (c) — Cheetahs from Namibia/South Africa were reintroduced at Kuno NP (Madhya Pradesh) in 2022 under NTCA. Gir hosts the Asiatic lion (Project Lion).

Prelims 2026 — anticipated themes

Likely: Newly launched or rebranded scheme mapping — PM E-DRIVE (successor to FAME-II), Amrit Dharohar (wetlands), GOBARdhan / compressed biogas, PM-Surya Ghar rooftop solar, or Green Credit Programme rules — matched to nodal ministry / objective. check for latest update or data

12. Mains PYQs + Model Answers

Questions below are original, exam-style formulations built from real UPSC GS-III themes — paraphrased to be copyright-safe. Each framework = intro angle → structured body → way-forward, with examples to quote.

Mains GS-III — style 2019 15 marks · 250 words

Q: Evaluate the effectiveness of the NAPCC and its eight missions in delivering India’s climate objectives.

Model Answer Framework
  1. Introduction — context: NAPCC 2008, 8 missions, complemented by SAPCCs.
  2. Achievements: Solar Mission scaling to 500 GW pathway; PAT under Energy Efficiency; Green India forestry sink.
  3. Gaps: uneven funding, weak SAPCC implementation, missions lagging (Himalayan, Water).
  4. Governance: coordination across ministries, MRV weaknesses.
  5. Link: Panchamrit, NDCs, net-zero 2070.
  6. Conclusion — way forward: outcome budgeting, updated missions, convergence with LiFE.
Mains GS-III — style 2021 15 marks · 250 words

Q: “Namami Gange marks a shift from pollution-abatement to integrated river-basin management.” Discuss.

Model Answer Framework
  1. Introduction — earlier failures: Ganga Action Plan I & II — fragmented, STP-only.
  2. Namami Gange shift: five pillars, basin approach, five-tier governance, EPA backing.
  3. Innovations: Hybrid Annuity Model STPs, real-time GPI monitoring, biodiversity (dolphin/gharial).
  4. Recognition: UN Top-10 restoration flagship.
  5. Challenges: tributary pollution, industrial effluent, sustaining flow (e-flow).
  6. Conclusion: integrated basin management as template for other rivers (NRCP).
Mains GS-III — style 2018 15 marks · 250 words

Q: Examine how India’s clean-air and wetland schemes complement its international environmental commitments.

Model Answer Framework
  1. Introduction — clean air: NCAP target, non-attainment cities, PRANA — links to WHO guidelines & SDG 11.
  2. Wetlands: Amrit Dharohar, NPCA, Ramsar sites — links to Ramsar Convention & CBD.
  3. Mangroves: MISHTI — blue carbon, coastal resilience (links to climate adaptation).
  4. Synergy: co-benefits for health, biodiversity, carbon sinks.
  5. Gaps: monitoring, funding, encroachment.
  6. Conclusion: domestic schemes operationalise treaty obligations.
Mains GS-III — style 2023 10 marks · 150 words

Q: Discuss the role of behavioural-change initiatives like Mission LiFE in climate mitigation.

Model Answer Framework
  1. Introduction — rationale: demand-side mitigation, mindful consumption vs supply-side targets.
  2. Mechanism: nudges, individual behaviour, P3 (pro-planet people).
  3. Global push: COP26/27, G20 2023.
  4. Critique: risk of shifting burden from state/industry to individuals; measurement hard.
  5. Conclusion: complement, not substitute, for structural decarbonisation.
Mains GS-III — style 2016 15 marks · 250 words

Q: “Species-recovery programmes have delivered conservation success but face habitat and human-wildlife conflict challenges.” Analyse with examples.

Model Answer Framework
  1. Introduction — successes: Project Tiger (~75% of wild tigers), Project Elephant, Asiatic lion recovery.
  2. New projects: Cheetah (Kuno), Lion, Dolphin.
  3. Challenges: habitat fragmentation, corridors, human-wildlife conflict, translocation risks (cheetah mortalities).
  4. Governance: NTCA, IDWH funding, community involvement.
  5. Way forward: landscape-level conservation, corridor protection, conflict compensation.
  6. Conclusion: move from single-species to ecosystem approach.
Mains GS-III — anticipated theme 15 marks · 250 words

Likely: Assess whether newer market/behavioural instruments — Green Credit Programme, Carbon Credit Trading Scheme, PM-Surya Ghar and Mission LiFE — can meaningfully accelerate India’s net-zero-2070 pathway beyond target-driven missions. check for latest update or data

15-Minute Revision Box

Must-Remember Facts — Indian Environmental Schemes & Missions

Missions, air & species
  • NAPCC (2008): 8 missions — Solar, Energy Efficiency, Habitat, Water, Himalayan, Green India, Agriculture, Strategic Knowledge. No Wind/Health mission in the classic 8.
  • Green India Mission: NAPCC Mission 6 (MoEFCC); + CAMPA (afforestation fund), Nagar Van, MISHTI (mangroves).
  • NCAP (2019): MoEFCC/CPCB; 40% PM cut by 2025-26; non-attainment cities; PRANA portal.
  • Species Projects: Tiger (1973, NTCA), Elephant (1992), Dolphin & Lion (2020), Cheetah (2022, Kuno).
  • Mission LiFE: 2022 (COP27), demand-side / P3 lifestyles, into G20 2023.
Rivers, energy & ministry traps
  • Namami Gange (2014): NMCG under Jal Shakti; National Ganga Council (PM); five pillars; EPA-backed; UN Top-10 flagship.
  • Swachh Bharat: Gramin (Jal Shakti), Urban (MoHUA); GOBARdhan (dung→biogas), SATAT (CBG).
  • Energy schemes: Solar Mission, PM-KUSUM, PM Surya Ghar, Green Hydrogen (MNRE); FAME/PM E-DRIVE (Heavy Industries).
  • Newest: Amrit Dharohar (wetlands) & MISHTI (mangroves) 2023; Green Credit Programme 2023.
  • Ministry traps: Namami Gange = Jal Shakti (not MoEFCC); FAME = Heavy Industries; NCAP = programme not Act.
Highest-frequency themes since 2014: NAPCC 8 missions & nodal ministry mapping; Namami Gange governance (NMCG/Jal Shakti); NCAP & non-attainment cities; species-recovery projects (Tiger, Cheetah-Kuno); and newest launches (MISHTI, Amrit Dharohar, Mission LiFE, Green Credit).
